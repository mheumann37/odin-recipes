Slow Cooker Buffalo Chicken Chili

Description

Heartier than soup but different than a traditional chili, this is fun to serve at a party, take along to a tailgate, or bring to a potluck. Ground chicken instead of beef and white beans instead of chili beans make up the base of this chili. Flavor from the chicken broth, ranch dressing mix, and fire-roasted tomatoes (along with the heat from the buffalo sauce) are balanced out with sweet corn and cream cheese. Top it off with fresh cilantro and blue cheese crumbles for the final touch.

Ingredients

  • Ground Chicken, 16 ounces
  • Drained & Rinsed White Navy Beans, 15 ounces
  • Drained Fire-Roasted Tomatoes, 14.5 ounces
  • Chicken Broth, 2 cups
  • Buffalo Wing Sauce, 1/4 - 1/2 cup
  • Ranch Dressing Mix, 1 packet
  • Frozen Corn Kernels, 1 cup
  • Onion Powder, 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garlic Powder, 1/2 teaspoon
  • Celery Salt, 1/2 teaspoon
  • Dried Cilantro, 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt, 1/4 teaspoon
  • Cream Cheese, 8 ounces
  • Blue Cheese Crumbles, Optional

Steps

  1. Brown ground chicken until fully cooked. Place in the slow cooker (or cook and refrigerate it until ready to fully assemble).
  2. Add remaining ingredients to slow cooker except for cream cheese and blue cheese. Stir to combine ingredients.
  3. Add block of cream cheese on top and cover with lid.
  4. Cook on high for 4 hours or on low for 8 hours.
  5. Stir to incorporate cream cheese and add additional buffalo wing sauce as desired.
  6. Top individual servings with blue cheese crumbles if desired.
