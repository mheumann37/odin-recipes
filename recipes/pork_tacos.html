One-Pan Pork Stir-Fry Tacos

Description

We are always up for a sweet and savory dinner pairing, and these tacos are just that. Ground pork and green pepper are cooked in a sweet soy glaze until sticky and tender. They are stuffed into steamy tortillas, along with a tangy, cilantro-flecked cabbage slaw. Of course, we would never skip out on taco toppings, and these ones do not disappoint: There is creamy, spicy Sriracha mayo and crispy fried onions. It is all ready in just 15 minutesâ€”life is sweet (and savory), friends.

Ingredients

  • Cilantro, 1/4 ounce
  • Rice Wine Vinegar, 5 teaspoons
  • Ground Pork, 10 ounces
  • Mayonnaise, 2 tablespoons
  • Flour Tortilla, 6 units
  • Coleslaw Mix, 4 ounces
  • Long Green Pepper, 1 unit
  • Sweet Soy Glaze, 4 tablespoons
  • Sriracha, 1 teaspoon
  • Crispy Fried Onions, 1 unit

Steps

  1. Wash and dry produce. Roughly chop cilantro. Halve, core, and thinly slice green pepper. In a medium bowl, toss together coleslaw mix, half the vinegar (all for 4 servings), half the cilantro, 1 tsp sugar (2 tsp for 4), and a pinch of salt until thoroughly combined.
  2. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add green pepper and cook until slightly softened, 2-3 minutes. Add pork and season with salt and pepper. Cook, breaking up meat into pieces, until browned and cooked through, 4-6 minutes. Stir in sweet soy glaze; cook until everything is coated and glaze has thickened, 1-2 minutes. Remove pan from heat.
  3. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine mayonnaise with Sriracha to taste. Season with salt and pepper. Wrap tortillas in damp paper towels and microwave until warm and pliable, 30 seconds.
  4. Divide tortillas between plates and fill with pork mixture and slaw (draining first). Top with Sriracha mayo, crispy fried onions, and remaining cilantro. Serve.
